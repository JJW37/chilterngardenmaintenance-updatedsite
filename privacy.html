Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Chiltern Garden Maintenance collects, uses and protects your personal data when you use this website or our services. We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Data Controller

Chiltern Garden Maintenance is the data controller responsible for your personal data within the meaning of GDPR Article 4(7). This means we decide how and why your personal data is processed.

If you have any questions about how we handle your data, you can contact us by:

  • Phone: 07467 657459
  • Email: hello@chilterngardenmaintenance.com
  • WhatsApp: message us
  • Post: arranged on request via the contact details above

We are not required to register with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) as we do not process personal data via automated means that would require registration, but we fully comply with GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 on a voluntary basis.

Data We Collect

Information you provide

When you contact us or use our quote form, we may collect:

  • Your name
  • Your phone number
  • Your email address (if you choose to provide it)
  • Your postcode and address
  • Details of the work you want done
  • Photos you send us of your garden
  • Your preferred timing for the work

Information collected automatically

If you accept analytics cookies on our cookie banner, we collect basic technical information via Google Analytics 4, including:

  • Your approximate location (city-level, derived from IP address with IP anonymisation enabled)
  • The pages you visit and how long you stay
  • The website that referred you to us (if any)
  • Basic device information (mobile, desktop, browser type)

If you decline analytics cookies, no analytics data is sent to Google. See our Cookie Policy for full details.

How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and provide quotes
  • Provide the garden services you have requested
  • Schedule and manage maintenance visits
  • Send you information about your service (appointment confirmations, visit reminders)
  • Keep records of work done at your property for warranty and quality purposes
  • Improve our website and services (via anonymous analytics, only with your consent)
  • Meet our legal and tax obligations

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data on the following legal bases under GDPR Article 6:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): to provide the services you have requested and to take steps at your request before entering into a contract (for example, preparing a quote).
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)): to respond to your enquiries, manage our business, keep records of work completed, and improve our website. Our legitimate interest is running a professional gardening business and providing the services you have asked us for.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): for analytics cookies (only if you click Accept on our cookie banner) and for any marketing communications (we do not currently send marketing).
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c)): to keep records for tax and HMRC purposes for the statutory retention period.

Data Retention

We keep your personal data only as long as necessary. Our retention periods are:

  • Quote enquiries that do not lead to work: 12 months from your last contact, then deleted.
  • Active client records: the duration of the working relationship plus 7 years for tax, legal and warranty purposes.
  • Photos of your garden: until the work is complete and any warranty period ends, then deleted unless you ask us to keep them for future reference.
  • Email and message correspondence: 24 months from the last message, then deleted.
  • Analytics data (Google Analytics): 14 months, after which Google automatically deletes it. This is the default GA4 retention period.
  • Cookie consent preference: 12 months, after which our cookie banner will reappear and ask for your preference again.

Data Sharing

We do not sell your data. We may share it with the following categories of recipient, only where necessary:

  • Waste disposal facilities: your address and the type of waste, for green waste disposal records required by the Environment Agency.
  • Specialist subcontractors: for example asbestos removal, tree surgery at height, or electrical work on outdoor lighting. We share only what is needed for them to quote and complete the work.
  • Our accountant: invoice and payment records for tax purposes, with no garden-specific details.
  • Google (analytics): if you accept analytics cookies, anonymous usage data is sent to Google Analytics. We have signed a Data Processing Agreement with Google and enabled IP anonymisation. Google does not use this data for its own purposes or share it with third parties for advertising.

We never share your data for marketing purposes, and we never sell it to anyone.

International Transfers

If you accept analytics cookies, your anonymised usage data is processed by Google in the United States. Google relies on the EU-US Data Privacy Framework for this transfer, which provides a lawful basis under GDPR. No personal data (such as your name or contact details) is sent to Google.

All other personal data we hold is stored and processed within the United Kingdom.

Your Rights

Under GDPR Articles 12 to 22, you have the following eight data subject rights:

  • Right to be informed: you have the right to know what data we collect and how we use it (this Privacy Policy fulfils that right).
  • Right of access: you can ask us for a copy of all the personal data we hold about you, free of charge, within one month.
  • Right to rectification: you can ask us to correct any inaccurate or incomplete data we hold about you.
  • Right to erasure: you can ask us to delete your personal data, subject to our legal obligations to keep some records (for example, tax records for 7 years).
  • Right to restrict processing: you can ask us to temporarily stop processing your data while a dispute is resolved.
  • Right to data portability: you can ask us to provide your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format so you can give it to another provider.
  • Right to object: you can object to us processing your data based on legitimate interests. We will stop unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: where we rely on your consent (analytics cookies), you can withdraw it at any time without giving a reason.

You also have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects. We do not use automated decision-making or profiling.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us. We will respond within one month of receiving your request.

Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect your personal data from unauthorised access, loss or damage. Specifically:

  • Access to your data is restricted to authorised personnel only (the business owner and named staff who need it to do your work).
  • Photos and client records are stored on password-protected devices.
  • We do not store payment card details. Payments are handled by bank transfer or card processor, never recorded by us.
  • We use HTTPS encryption on this website so data in transit cannot be intercepted.
  • If we become aware of a data breach affecting your personal data, we will notify you and the ICO within 72 hours as required by GDPR Article 33 and 34.

Children's Data

Our services are aimed at adult homeowners and property managers. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 18. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date at the top. We will not reduce your rights under this policy without notifying you.

Complaints

If you have a concern about how we handle your personal data, please contact us first and we will do our best to resolve it. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK's independent data protection regulator:

  • Website: ico.org.uk
  • Phone: 0303 123 1113
  • Post: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire SK9 5AF
