Garden Analysis Report

Find out why your lawn or plants are dying with our detailed analysis report.

What Is a Garden Analysis Report?

If your lawn is going brown, your plants keep dying, or your borders look tired no matter what you do, a Garden Analysis Report will tell you why. We visit your garden, take soil samples, assess the conditions, and produce a detailed report explaining what is wrong and how to fix it.

This is a diagnostic service. We do not carry out any work during the visit. We just diagnose the problems and give you a clear, written plan to fix them. You can then fix the problems yourself, buy one of our Chiltern Guides for step by step instructions, or hire us to carry out the work.

What the Report Includes
  • Soil analysis: pH test, soil type identification, nutrient assessment, drainage test
  • Lawn assessment: thatch depth, compaction test, moss identification, weed identification, grass species identification
  • Plant assessment: identification of failing plants, diagnosis of causes (wrong soil, wrong light, wrong watering, pest or disease)
  • Light and shade survey: mapping of sun and shade through the day
  • Drainage assessment: identification of waterlogging issues and drainage problems
  • Pest and disease check: identification of common garden pests and diseases
  • Written report: a detailed PDF report explaining every problem found and the recommended fix
  • Action plan: a prioritised list of jobs to fix the problems, with estimated costs
Common Problems We Diagnose

Lawn problems

  • Moss taking over (causes: shade, compaction, poor drainage, acidic soil)
  • Bare patches (causes: dog urine, heavy use, shade, disease, drought)
  • Yellow or pale grass (causes: nutrient deficiency, wrong pH, drought)
  • Weeds taking over (causes: thin grass, bare patches, wrong mowing height)

Plant problems

  • Plants dying after planting (causes: wrong depth, wrong soil, wrong light, root damage)
  • Established plants declining (causes: soil exhaustion, competition, pest or disease)
  • Yellow leaves (causes: nutrient deficiency, waterlogging, drought)
  • No flowers (causes: wrong pruning, wrong feeding, wrong light)

Soil problems

  • Wrong pH for the plants you are growing
  • Poor drainage causing root rot
  • Compacted soil preventing root growth
  • Depleted soil with no organic matter
  • Chalky soil causing nutrient lock-up
  • Clay soil holding too much water in winter
The Process
  1. Booking: contact us to book a site visit
  2. Site visit (1 to 2 hours): we walk the garden, take soil samples, test pH, assess the lawn and plants, and photograph the problems
  3. Sample review and diagnostic assessment (5 to 7 working days): we review the soil samples and identify any pest or disease issues
  4. Report production (3 to 5 working days): we write up the full report with photos, diagnosis and action plan
  5. Report delivery: we send you the PDF report by email and discuss it with you by phone

FAQs
How long does the analysis take?

The site visit takes 1 to 2 hours. The full report is delivered within 7 to 10 working days.

Do I get a written report?

Yes, you receive a detailed PDF report with photos, diagnosis and an action plan.

Can the report be used for insurance claims?

The report provides a written record of observed garden conditions and identified problems. It may be useful when discussing damage with insurers or other parties. It is not an expert witness report unless separately commissioned and agreed.

Do you carry out the recommended work?

Yes, we can quote for any work recommended in the report, or you can use the report to do the work yourself.

What areas do you cover for analysis?
