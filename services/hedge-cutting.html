Hedge Cutting

Formal and informal hedges cut and shaped at the right time of year.

Hedge Cutting Services

A well-maintained hedge is one of the most valuable features in any garden. It gives privacy, structure and shelter for wildlife. Our hedge cutting service covers all species and sizes, from small box edging to tall beech and leylandii hedges.

  • Annual and biannual formal hedge cuts
  • Informal hedge pruning
  • Hedge height reduction
  • Hedge renovation for overgrown hedges
  • Hedge removal and stump treatment
  • Tall hedge cutting with platforms and ladders
Species We Cut

We cut all common UK hedge species, including:

  • Beech (Fagus sylvatica): cut once a year in late August
  • Hornbeam (Carpinus betulus): similar to beech
  • Yew (Taxus baccata): formal hedges cut twice a year
  • Leylandii: cut twice to keep in check
  • Privet: cut 2 to 3 times through the growing season
  • Laurel (Prunus laurocerasus): cut once in late summer
  • Hawthorn: native hedge, cut in February
  • Box (Buxus): formal low hedges, cut in May and September
  • Escallonia and Pittosporum: mild climate hedges

For full timings and rules, see our article on when to cut hedges in the UK.

Bird Nesting Season

Under the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981, it is an offence to damage or destroy the nest of any wild bird while it is being built or used. The main nesting season is 1 March to 31 August. We can technically cut hedges during this period but we always check carefully for nests first. If in doubt, we wait until September.

See RSPB hedge cutting guidance for more detail.

Hedge Renovation

Overgrown hedges can usually be renovated rather than removed. Yew, beech, hornbeam, hawthorn, privet and laurel all respond well to hard pruning. We cut one side one year and the other side the next, to reduce the shock to the plant and keep some screening while it regrows.

Leylandii cannot be cut back into old brown wood. If a leylandii hedge has got too tall or too wide, the only option is to remove it and replant with something more manageable.

Waste Removal

All hedge cuttings are taken away. We always leave the garden clean and tidy.

FAQs
How often should hedges be cut?

It depends on species and desired finish. Many domestic hedges need at least one cut per year; faster-growing hedges may need more frequent trimming.

Can you reduce hedge height?

Light to moderate reductions may be possible depending on hedge type and access. Send photos first.

Do you remove clippings?

This depends on the quote and volume of cuttings. Please ask when booking.

Can hedge cutting be included in regular maintenance?

Often yes, where appropriate and planned in advance.

Are there nesting bird restrictions?

Timing and method may need adjusting where nesting birds are present. We check conditions before work.
