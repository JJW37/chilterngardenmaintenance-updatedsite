The short answer

Quick Answer

The cost of pressure washing a patio in 2026 ranges from 120 to 350 pounds for a standard 40 sqm patio. Small patios (20 sqm) cost 120 to 220 pounds. Large driveways (100 sqm) cost 450 to 750 pounds. DIY costs 50 to 100 pounds for pressure washer hire. In the Chilterns, prices are at the higher end due to higher labour rates.

How Much Does Pressure Washing Cost in 2026?

Pressure washing is priced by area, with a minimum charge of around 100 pounds. The cost varies depending on the size of the area, the surface type, the level of soiling, and whether waste removal is included. In the Chilterns, where labour rates are 40 to 50 pounds per hour, pressure washing is at the higher end of the UK price range.

What Affects the Cost?

Several factors affect the cost of pressure washing:

1. Size of the Area

Most contractors charge per square metre, with a minimum charge. The cost per square metre decreases as the area increases.

2. Surface Type

Hard surfaces (concrete, block paving) are cheaper to clean than soft stones (Indian sandstone, limestone) because they can take higher pressure. Soft stones need lower pressure and more care.

3. Level of Soiling

Heavily soiled patios with moss, algae, and stains take longer to clean and cost more. Lightly soiled patios are cheaper.

4. Stain Treatment

Oil stains, rust stains, and organic stains need chemical treatment before pressure washing. This adds 50 to 150 pounds to the cost.

5. Re-sanding Block Paving

Pressure washing removes the joint sand from block paving. Re-sanding with kiln-dried sand is essential. This adds 50 to 100 pounds to the cost.

6. Sealing

Sealing the surface after cleaning keeps it cleaner for longer. Sealing adds 5 to 10 pounds per square metre, or 200 to 500 pounds for a typical patio.

7. Waste Removal

Waste water and debris must be collected and disposed of. Most contractors include this in the price, but check.

8. Access

Difficult access (rear garden with no side access, narrow lanes) adds to the cost.

How to Save Money on Pressure Washing

  • Combine with neighbours: some contractors offer discounts for multiple properties in the same street
  • Book in spring or autumn: the busy season is summer. Book off-peak for lower prices.
  • Skip sealing: sealing is optional. Skip it if budget is tight.
  • Choose the right surface: concrete is cheaper to clean than Indian sandstone
  • Maintain regularly: an annual clean is cheaper than letting the patio get badly soiled

Common Mistakes

  • Pressure washing too often. Pressure washing wears the surface. Once a year is enough.
  • Using too high a pressure. High pressure damages sandstone, tarmac and pointing. Use the right pressure.
  • Not re-sanding block paving. Always re-sand with kiln-dried sand after pressure washing.
  • Not treating stains first. Pressure washing alone will not remove oil or rust stains.
  • Choosing the cheapest quote. Cheap contractors may not have insurance or may damage the surface.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does pressure washing cost per square metre?

5 to 8 pounds per square metre for hard surfaces (concrete, block paving), 7 to 12 pounds per square metre for soft stones (Indian sandstone, limestone).

Is DIY pressure washing worth it?

For small areas (under 20 sqm), yes. For larger areas, the time and effort make professional cleaning better value.

How long does pressure washing take?

2 to 4 hours for a 40 sqm patio. 4 to 8 hours for a 100 sqm driveway.
