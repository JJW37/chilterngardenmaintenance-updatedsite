How Often Should a Gardener Visit?

One of the most common questions we get asked is how often a gardener should visit. The answer depends on the garden, the season, the budget and the standard you want. This guide explains the trade-offs of each frequency and helps you choose the right rhythm for your garden.

The short answer

Quick Answer

For most UK family gardens, fortnightly visits from March to October and monthly visits from November to February works well. This keeps the garden looking good without breaking the bank. Smaller courtyard gardens can be monthly year round. Large or formal gardens may need weekly visits in the growing season.

The Three Main Options

Weekly visits

Weekly visits are for formal gardens, large estates, and serious gardeners who want the garden to look immaculate every day. The lawn is cut every week without fail, borders are weeded before weeds are visible, and hedges are kept perfectly shaped.

Pros:

  • Garden always looks pristine
  • Smaller jobs each visit, easier to keep on top
  • Problems spotted quickly

Cons:

  • Most expensive option, typically 200 to 600 per month
  • Not necessary for most family gardens
  • Some jobs only need doing monthly anyway

Fortnightly visits

The sweet spot for most UK gardens. The lawn is cut every two weeks, which is the right interval for most of the growing season. Weeds are caught before they get big. Hedges are checked regularly.

Pros:

  • Good balance of cost and quality
  • Lawn cut at the right interval for most of the year
  • Most family gardens thrive on this schedule

Cons:

  • Lawn can look slightly long by week 2 in peak growing season
  • Some fast-growing weeds may need pulling between visits

Monthly visits

Monthly visits work for small courtyard gardens, low-maintenance gardens, and through the winter. A monthly visit can keep on top of things but the lawn will need cutting between visits in summer.

Pros:

  • Cheapest option, typically 40 to 150 per month
  • Fine for low-maintenance gardens
  • Perfect for winter when growth is slow

Cons:

  • Lawn grows too long between visits in summer
  • Weeds can get established before they are caught
  • Borders can look untidy by week 3

How to Choose

Garden size

Larger gardens need more frequent visits because more goes wrong between visits. A 50 square metre courtyard garden can be kept tidy monthly. A 500 square metre garden with extensive borders needs fortnightly as a minimum.

Lawn area

The lawn is the main driver of visit frequency. Lawns need cutting every 7 to 10 days in peak growing season (May to July). If you have a lawn, fortnightly is the minimum through summer.

Standard of finish

If you want the garden to look like a National Trust property, you need weekly visits. If you want it to look presentable and well cared for, fortnightly is enough. If you just want it not to be a jungle, monthly will do.

Budget

Be honest about what you can afford. A fortnightly visit at 50 pounds per visit is 100 pounds per month, or 1,200 per year. Weekly at the same rate is 2,400 per year. Monthly is 600 per year. See our maintenance cost guide for more detail.

Your own time and interest

If you enjoy gardening and want to do some yourself, monthly visits from a professional to handle the heavy work can be enough. If you would rather never touch the garden, fortnightly or weekly is better.

Seasonal Variations

March to May (spring)

Fortnightly is ideal. The garden is growing fast and needs regular attention. Lawn needs its first cuts, borders need clearing and weeding, hedges need their first cut.

June to August (summer)

Fortnightly for most gardens. Weekly for formal lawns. The lawn grows fastest in May and June, then slows in dry July and August.

September to November (autumn)

Monthly is usually enough. Growth is slowing. Leaf clearance is the main job. Lawn still needs cutting but less frequently.

December to February (winter)

Monthly or even less. Most gardens need very little through winter. A tidy in January to clear dead growth and check structures is enough.

Special Cases

Lawns

If you have a lawn, you need fortnightly visits at minimum through the growing season, even if the rest of the garden is low maintenance. Lawns grow too fast for monthly visits. Either cut the lawn yourself between professional visits, or arrange weekly cuts through May, June and July.

Hedges

Hedge cutting is a separate job, not part of regular maintenance. Most hedges need cutting once or twice a year. Book this as a one-off job. See our hedge cutting guide.

Containers and pots

Pots need daily watering in summer. If you are away or forget, the plants die. Either install drip irrigation on a timer, or arrange for someone to water daily through the hottest months.

Newly planted gardens

New plants need regular watering through their first growing season. Weekly visits to check and water are sensible for the first year.

Vegetable gardens

Vegetable gardens need attention every few days in summer. Pests, watering, harvesting and sowing successional crops all need doing. Most maintenance gardeners do not specialise in vegetable growing.

Signs Your Schedule Is Wrong

Signs you need more frequent visits

  • Lawn looks long between visits
  • Weeds are flowering before they are pulled
  • Hedges are losing their shape
  • Paths and patios are growing green algae
  • You are doing significant work between visits

Signs you need less frequent visits

  • The gardener is finishing in 30 minutes
  • There is nothing for them to do in winter
  • Your garden is mostly hard landscaping
  • You are doing the work yourself between visits

Flexibility

A good gardener should be flexible. You should be able to add an extra visit before a party or a house sale, and skip a visit when you are away or the garden does not need it. We allow clients to pause in winter and add visits in summer without penalty.

Final Thoughts

Choosing the right visit frequency is about balance. Too infrequent and the garden looks scruffy between visits. Too frequent and you are paying for work that does not need doing. Most UK family gardens thrive on fortnightly visits from March to October and monthly through winter.
