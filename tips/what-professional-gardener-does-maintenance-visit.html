The short answer

Quick Answer

A professional gardener does on a maintenance visit: mow and edge the lawn, weed the borders, deadhead flowering plants, light pruning, sweep paths and patios, check for pests and diseases, and report any issues. A typical maintenance visit lasts 2 to 4 hours for a medium garden. In the Chilterns, a maintenance gardener also advises on soil-specific issues (chalk vs clay) and seasonal jobs.

What a Professional Gardener Actually Does

Many people hire a gardener without knowing what they actually do. The result is often disappointment: the gardener does not do what the client expected, or the client expects services the gardener does not provide. This guide explains what a professional gardener does on a typical maintenance visit, so you know what to expect.

In the Chilterns, where gardens range from small courtyard plots to large estates, maintenance visits vary enormously. But the core jobs are the same. This guide lists what we do on a typical maintenance visit, with notes on what is included and what is not.

The Standard Maintenance Visit

A standard maintenance visit includes:

1. Lawn Mowing and Edging

Mow the lawn to the right height for the season (25 to 40mm in summer, 40 to 50mm in spring and autumn). Edge along paths and borders with a strimmer or edging shears. Sweep up the clippings.

2. Border Weeding

Hand weed the borders to get the roots out, not just the tops. Hoe annual weeds on dry days. Dig out perennial weeds (bindweed, couch grass, ground elder) with a fork.

3. Deadheading

Deadhead flowering plants to encourage more flowers. Roses, perennials, annuals all benefit from regular deadheading.

4. Light Pruning

Light pruning of shrubs and perennials. This includes:

  • Cutting back dead flowers
  • Trimming wayward shoots
  • Removing dead or diseased wood
  • Cutting back perennials that have finished flowering

Major pruning (of mature shrubs and trees) is usually quoted separately.

5. Hedge Trimming (in season)

Trim small hedges (under 2 metres) with a hedge trimmer. Large hedges are usually quoted separately.

6. Sweeping Paths and Patios

Sweep paths, patios and driveways. Removes leaves, debris and weeds.

7. Checking for Pests and Diseases

Walk the garden and check for pests and diseases. Common problems to look for:

  • Aphids on roses and perennials
  • Blackspot on roses
  • Lily beetle on lilies
  • Box moth on box
  • Slugs and snails on hostas
  • Vine weevil on rhododendrons

Report any problems to the client via WhatsApp with photos.

8. Waste Removal

All green waste is taken away.

9. Reporting

At the end of the visit, the gardener reports on what was done and any issues spotted. This is usually via WhatsApp with photos.

What Is NOT Included in a Standard Maintenance Visit

  • Tree surgery: large tree work needs a specialist contractor
  • Lawn renovation: scarification, aeration, overseeding
  • Hard landscaping: patio, fence, deck work
  • Planting schemes: quoted separately as soft landscaping
  • Major pruning: of mature shrubs and trees
  • Pest and disease treatment: quoted as needed
  • Hedge cutting over 2 metres: usually quoted separately
  • Pressure washing: quoted separately

Common Mistakes

  • Not having a clear agreement. Agree what is included and what is not before starting.
  • Not communicating. If you want something done differently, tell the gardener. Vague instructions lead to disappointment.
  • Expecting the gardener to do everything. Tree surgery, lawn renovation and hard landscaping are usually separate.
  • Not being clear about access. Make sure the gardener can get in on the agreed day.
  • Not reporting problems. If you are not happy with the work, tell the gardener immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be home for the maintenance visit?

No. Most clients are not home. Provide access via a side gate or key safe, and the gardener will work and leave a WhatsApp report.

How long does a maintenance visit take?

2 to 4 hours for a medium garden, 4 to 8 hours for a large garden. See our visit frequency guide.

What if I am not happy with the work?

Tell your gardener immediately. Most issues are communication problems, not competence problems. A quick WhatsApp with a photo is usually all it takes.
