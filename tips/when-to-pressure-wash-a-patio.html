When to Pressure Wash a Patio

Pressure washing transforms a green, slippery patio into a clean, safe surface. But timing matters. Do it at the wrong time of year and the results will not last. This guide explains when to pressure wash a patio in the UK for the best, longest-lasting results.

The short answer

Quick Answer

The best time to pressure wash a patio is in late spring (April to May) or early autumn (September to October). Avoid pressure washing in winter (frost can damage surfaces) or in high summer (water evaporates too fast, leaving tide marks). Most patios need pressure washing once a year. In the Chilterns, where winters are wet and patios get green with algae, an annual wash in April or May is ideal.

Why Timing Matters

Pressure washing at the right time gives better results and avoids damaging the surface. The wrong time can leave tide marks, damage the surface, or cause efflorescence (white salt deposits). In the Chilterns, where the climate is wet and patios are prone to algae, timing is particularly important.

The Best Time to Pressure Wash

Late Spring (April to May)

This is the ideal time. The winter algae and grime have built up, the weather is warm enough to dry the patio quickly, and you have the whole summer to enjoy the clean patio. Avoid pressure washing in March if there is still a risk of frost.

Early Autumn (September to October)

The second best time. The summer is over, the patio has accumulated grime from BBQs and foot traffic, and you want it clean before winter. Avoid October if frost is forecast, as water in cracks can freeze and damage the surface.

When NOT to Pressure Wash

Winter (November to March)

Avoid pressure washing in winter. Frost can damage the surface, especially sandstone and limestone. Water in cracks can freeze and split the stone. The patio will not dry properly, leaving tide marks. If you must pressure wash in winter, choose a mild, dry day with no frost forecast for 48 hours.

High Summer (July to August)

Avoid pressure washing in the heat of summer. Water evaporates too fast, leaving tide marks and dissolved salts on the surface. If you must pressure wash in summer, do it early in the morning or in the evening when it is cooler.

During or After Rain

The patio should be dry before you start. A wet patio will not show you which areas need cleaning, and the water will not penetrate the dirt. Wait 24 to 48 hours after rain before pressure washing.

During Bird Nesting Season (If Near Hedges)

If your patio is near a hedge with nesting birds, the noise of the pressure washer can disturb them. Avoid pressure washing between March and August if nesting birds are present.

How Often to Pressure Wash

Most patios need pressure washing once a year. Patios in shaded, damp positions (under trees, north-facing) may need washing twice a year. Patios in sunny, well-ventilated positions may only need washing every 18 months.

Signs your patio needs pressure washing:

  • Green algae on the surface
  • Black or green staining
  • Slippery when wet
  • Weeds growing in the joints
  • Visible dirt and grime

The Pressure Washing Process

  1. Clear the area: remove furniture, pots, and obstacles.
  2. Sweep: remove loose dirt and leaves.
  3. Treat stains: apply a chemical cleaner for oil, rust, or organic stains.
  4. Test a small area: check the pressure does not damage the surface.
  5. Pressure wash: work in sections, keep the lance moving.
  6. Re-sand block paving: brush kiln-dried sand into the joints.
  7. Allow to dry: wait 24 to 48 hours before replacing furniture.
  8. Consider sealing: a sealant keeps the surface cleaner for longer.

Common Mistakes

  • Pressure washing too often. Pressure washing wears the surface. Once a year is enough for most patios.
  • Using too high a pressure. High pressure damages sandstone, tarmac and pointing. Use the right pressure for the surface.
  • Not re-sanding block paving. Pressure washing removes the joint sand. Always re-sand with kiln-dried sand.
  • Pressure washing in winter. Frost can damage the surface. Wait for spring or autumn.
  • Not treating stains first. Oil and rust stains need chemical treatment before pressure washing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I pressure wash in the rain?

No. The patio should be dry before you start. A wet patio will not show you which areas need cleaning, and the water will not penetrate the dirt.

How long does a pressure washed patio stay clean?

6 to 12 months, depending on shade, dampness, and foot traffic. Shaded, damp patios go green faster. Sealing the patio after washing keeps it cleaner for longer.

Will pressure washing damage my patio?

Not if done correctly. Use the right pressure for the surface, keep the lance moving, and do not get too close. Soft stones like sandstone and limestone need low pressure.
