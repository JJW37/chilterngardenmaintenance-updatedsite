Garden Waste Removal Costs: What Affects the Price?

Garden waste removal is often the hidden cost of any clearance or tidy-up. The cutting and digging is the visible work, but getting rid of the waste can add 20 to 40 percent to the bill. This guide explains what garden waste removal costs in the UK in 2026, the different options available, and how to keep the price down.

The short answer

Quick Answer

Garden waste removal in the UK in 2026 costs between 120 and 200 per tonne including transport and disposal. For a typical family garden clearance producing 1 to 2 tonnes, expect 120 to 400. For larger clearances producing 4 to 6 tonnes, expect 480 to 1,200.

Garden waste removal options

Man with van (per tonne): 120 to 200
Skip hire (6 yard): 200 to 350
Hippobag (1 tonne megabag): 150 to 200
Council green waste bin (annual): 60 to 100
Trip to council tip (free but time): 0

What Affects the Cost of Garden Waste Removal

Volume of waste

The biggest factor. Waste is charged per tonne. A standard family garden clearance produces 1 to 2 tonnes. A heavy clearance of a neglected garden can produce 4 to 6 tonnes.

Type of waste

Green waste (leaves, grass, branches) is cheapest to dispose of. Mixed waste (green waste plus soil, rubble, timber) is more expensive because it has to be sorted. Soil and rubble are classed as inert waste and are cheaper per tonne but heavier.

Access

If we can get a tipper truck to the garden, loading is quick. If waste has to be barrowed through the house or up a long path, loading takes longer and costs more.

Distance to the disposal site

The further the garden is from a licensed disposal site, the higher the transport cost. In the Chilterns, most gardens are within 20 minutes of a disposal site, so transport is not a major factor.

Waste Removal Options Compared

Man with van (us)

We bring a tipper truck, load the waste, and take it to a licensed disposal site. You get a record of the disposal. Cost: 120 to 200 per tonne. Best for: any clearance where you want us to handle everything.

Skip hire

You hire a skip, we fill it, the skip company collects it. Cost: 200 to 350 for a 6 yard skip (holds about 3 tonnes). You need a permit if the skip goes on the road (30 to 80). Best for: large clearances where you have space for a skip.

Hippobag

A large bag you fill yourself, then a company collects it. Cost: 150 to 200 for a 1 tonne megabag. Best for: small clearances where you want to do the loading yourself.

Council green waste bin

An annual subscription to your council green waste scheme. Cost: 60 to 100 per year. Best for: ongoing light waste from regular maintenance. Too slow for clearance work.

Trip to the council tip

Free for household waste, but most tips limit you to 6 bags per visit. A typical clearance produces 20 to 40 bags. That means 4 to 7 trips. Best for: small clearances if you have a car and time.

How to Reduce Waste Removal Costs

Have a bonfire

Burning dry woody waste is still allowed in most areas (check local rules). A bonfire can reduce 2 tonnes of bramble cuttings to a bucket of ash. Best done in late autumn or winter when neighbours have windows shut.

Compost at home

Leaves, grass clippings and soft green waste can be composted. A compost heap or bin takes 6 to 12 months to break down, but the result is free compost for the garden. See our compost guide.

Chip woody waste

Branches and woody cuttings can be chipped and used as mulch. We can bring a chipper for an additional 100 to 200 per day.

Separate soil and rubble

Soil and rubble are classed as inert waste and are cheaper to dispose of (80 to 120 per tonne) than green waste. If you have a mixed clearance, separating soil and rubble saves money.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I put garden waste in my black bin?

No. Most councils do not accept garden waste in the general waste bin. You need a green waste bin, a trip to the tip, or a paid removal service.

How much waste does a typical clearance produce?

A small garden clearance produces 0.5 to 1 tonne. A medium garden produces 1 to 3 tonnes. A large garden clearance can produce 4 to 6 tonnes or more.

Do you give me proof of disposal?

Yes. We provide a record of disposal for every load. This is your proof that the waste was disposed of legally.
