Large gardens need a different approach to maintenance than small ones. The scale means more planning, more equipment and a more structured schedule. This guide explains what should be included in regular maintenance for large UK gardens.

The short answer

Quick Answer

Regular maintenance for large gardens in the Chilterns includes: weekly or fortnightly visits, lawn mowing, border weeding, hedge trimming in season, path and patio sweeping, debris removal, and reporting of issues. Large gardens (0.5 to 5 acres) typically need 4 to 8 hours per visit . A formal maintenance plan keeps the garden looking good year-round.

Why Large Gardens Need Regular Maintenance

Large gardens need regular maintenance more than small gardens do. The scale means that a missed visit shows. A small garden that is a week late looks untidy. A large garden that is a week late looks abandoned. The sheer volume of growth, the number of plants, the length of hedges and the size of lawns all mean that regular maintenance is essential to keep the garden looking good.

Large gardens also have more complex maintenance needs. There are usually multiple zones (formal garden, vegetable garden, orchard, woodland, paddock), each with different requirements. There are usually more mature trees, longer hedges, bigger lawns, and more infrastructure (fences, paths, outbuildings). A regular maintenance gardener who knows the garden can keep on top of all this. An ad-hoc approach cannot.

This guide explains what regular maintenance for large gardens in the Chilterns includes, what it costs, and how to set up a maintenance plan that works.

What Regular Maintenance Includes

A typical maintenance visit for a large garden in the Chilterns includes:

  • Lawn mowing and edging: weekly in the growing season, monthly in winter. Includes ride-on mowing for large lawns.
  • Border weeding and tidying: hand weeding to get the roots out, deadheading, light pruning.
  • Hedge trimming: in season (August to September for most hedges, January for native hedges).
  • Path and patio sweeping: keeps hard surfaces clean and safe.
  • Leaf clearance: weekly in autumn.
  • Debris removal: all green waste taken away.
  • Reporting: we WhatsApp photos of any issues we spot (pests, diseases, broken fences, leaks).

Things usually NOT included in a standard maintenance visit:

  • Tree surgery (large tree work needs a specialist contractor)
  • Lawn renovation (scarification, aeration, overseeding)
  • Hard landscaping (patio, fence, deck work)
  • Planting schemes (quoted separately as soft landscaping)
  • Major pruning of mature shrubs and trees
  • Pest and disease treatment (quoted as needed)

How Often Should We Visit?

The frequency depends on the size and style of the garden.

  • Weekly visits: for formal gardens, large lawns, and gardens with high standards. March to October.
  • Fortnightly visits: for most large family gardens. March to October.
  • Monthly visits: for low-maintenance gardens and all gardens in winter.

Setting Up a Maintenance Plan

A formal maintenance plan is essential for a large garden. The plan should include:

  • Visit frequency (weekly, fortnightly, monthly)
  • What is included in each visit (lawn, borders, hedges, paths, etc.)
  • What is NOT included (tree work, lawn renovation, hard landscaping)
  • Seasonal jobs (lawn renovation in September, hedge cutting in August, leaf clearance in October)
  • Communication (WhatsApp, email, monthly report)
  • Access arrangements (side gate, key safe, dog)
  • Payment terms (monthly invoice, direct debit)

Common Mistakes with Large Garden Maintenance

  • Not having a plan. Without a plan, jobs are missed and the garden deteriorates.
  • Not visiting often enough. A large garden needs weekly or fortnightly visits. Monthly is not enough in the growing season.
  • Not communicating. The gardener needs to know what you want. Vague instructions lead to disappointment.
  • Not budgeting for seasonal jobs. Hedge cutting, lawn renovation and leaf clearance are extra costs on top of regular maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does maintenance cost for a 2 acre garden?

Typically 3,000 to 5,000 pounds per year for weekly maintenance from March to October, plus 500 to 1,000 pounds for winter maintenance. See our garden care level assessment for a CGM Care Level assessment.

Do I need a regular gardener or can I do it myself?

A large garden is too much for one person to maintain alone. Most large garden owners do the easy bits themselves and hire a professional for the heavy work. See our weekly vs fortnightly guide.

What is included in a maintenance visit?

Lawn mowing, border weeding, deadheading, light pruning, path sweeping, leaf clearance, and waste removal. See our CGM Method page.
