Garden Services in Wraysbury

Wraysbury sits on the Thames near Windsor, with a mix of period properties and family homes. The soil is mostly river gravel and alluvium, free-draining and fertile.

Local conditions in Wraysbury

Geology and soil

River gravels and alluvium on the Thames floodplain, with London Clay beneath.

Soil type: Free-draining sandy loam on the gravel terraces. Heavier alluvial soil near the river.

Soil pH: Neutral to slightly acidic, pH 6.0 to 7.0.

Rainfall

Annual rainfall: Approximately 650mm per year.

Monthly pattern: Wettest October to January at 60 to 65mm. Driest July and August at 40mm.

Temperature

Annual average 10.8C. Summer highs 23C. Winter lows 2C.

Garden types in the area

Period riverside properties, family homes, properties near the lakes.

Local conditions to consider

Riverside gardens can flood. Gravel soils drain freely but can be drought-prone in summer.

Seasonal issues specific to Wraysbury

These are the seasonal issues we see most often in Wraysbury gardens, drawn from our local work and the town's soil and climate profile.

  • Fast-draining sandy soil in Wraysbury dries out quickly in summer - mulch heavily in spring and install water butts
  • Nutrient leaching on sandy soil - feed little and often rather than one heavy dose, as nutrients wash through
  • High waterlogging risk in Wraysbury - check for standing water after rain and consider French drains or raised beds in low spots
