Local conditions in Flackwell Heath

Geology and soil

Chalk on the escarpment slopes, clay-with-flints on the plateau at the top.

Soil type: Shallow free-draining chalky loam on the slopes. Heavy clay-loam on the plateau.

Soil pH: Alkaline on the chalk slopes, pH 7.5 to 8.0. Neutral on the plateau, pH 6.5 to 7.0.

Rainfall

Annual rainfall: Approximately 690mm per year.

Monthly pattern: Wettest October to January at 65 to 70mm. Driest July and August at 45mm.

Temperature

Annual average 10.5C. Summer highs 22C. Winter lows 1C. Exposed on the escarpment.

Garden types in the area

Period properties on the slopes, family homes on the plateau, modern developments around the village.

Local conditions to consider

Steep slopes make maintenance challenging. Chalky soil suits Mediterranean plants. Exposed position means wind can be an issue.

Seasonal issues specific to Flackwell Heath

These are the seasonal issues we see most often in Flackwell Heath gardens, drawn from our local work and the town's soil and climate profile.

  • Spring drought stress on chalk soils in Flackwell Heath - water new plantings weekly through the first summer, as chalk drains faster than most gardeners expect
  • Alkaline soil (pH 7.5-8) limits acid-loving plants - rhododendrons, camellias and blueberries will struggle without ericaceous containers or imported soil
  • Shallow soil depth on chalk escarpment around Flackwell Heath - large trees may need staking longer and watering more in dry spells
  • Free-draining conditions around Flackwell Heath - lawns may need supplemental irrigation in July and August
