A typical project

From overgrown to ongoing, in six weeks.

When a garden has been left, the work is rarely just clearance. Brambles have shaded out the lawn. Moss has moved in where grass has given up. Shrubs have grown leggy reaching for light. The fence is half-eaten.

We clear it once, properly. Then we put it on a plan that keeps it improving, not just holding. Most overgrown gardens we see are back to a state you'd be happy to sit in within four to six weeks of starting.

Project: Thame, Oxfordshire. Clearance, lawn recovery, border replanting, fence restoration. Six weeks of work, now on a fortnightly maintenance plan.

PRINCIPLED METHOD · GARDEN CARE

A garden is much more than decoration. It is the soul of your home, a perfect reflection of your character.

We send gardeners that we would only send to our own members of family.

Yew hedge, hand-pruned. Side profile, mid-winter. Working quietly is part of the job.

How we actually work

Right plant.Right place.Right time.Right job.

A plant in the wrong place will always struggle. A job done at the wrong time of year will undo itself. We choose plants that suit the soil, light and moisture of each spot in the garden, and we do each job when it will actually take.

A mulch in spring saves hours of weeding in summer. A regular feed keeps plants healthier than a panic feed when they're struggling. If a plant is past saving, we'll say so. If a cheaper option exists, we'll tell you about it.
