If you love entertaining outdoors, your garden needs to be designed for it. That means comfortable seating areas, good lighting, privacy from neighbours, and plants that create the right atmosphere. This guide explains garden design for entertaining.

Why Design for Entertaining

A garden designed for entertaining extends the living space of the house. It provides a place to eat, drink, socialise, and relax outdoors. In the Chilterns, where summers are warm and properties are often used for entertaining, a well-designed entertaining garden is a valuable feature.

The key is to create distinct zones for different activities, use comfortable and durable furniture, install good lighting, and provide privacy. This guide explains how to design a garden for entertaining, with specific ideas for Chiltern gardens.

The 5 Elements of an Entertaining Garden

1. Distinct Zones

Create distinct zones for different activities:

  • Dining zone: near the house, with a table and chairs
  • Lounging zone: comfortable seating, perhaps a fire pit
  • Cooking zone: outdoor kitchen or barbecue
  • Drinks zone: a small seating area for pre-dinner drinks

2. Comfortable Seating

Invest in comfortable, durable outdoor seating:

  • Outdoor sofas and armchairs with weatherproof cushions
  • Dining table and chairs for 6 to 10 people
  • Sun loungers for the lawn
  • Bench seating for flexibility

3. Outdoor Lighting

Lighting is essential for evening entertaining:

  • String lights across the patio
  • Path lights for safety
  • Uplighting on specimen trees
  • Wall lights on the house
  • Candles or lanterns on tables

4. Privacy

Provide privacy for entertaining:

  • Hedges: beech, yew, or hornbeam
  • Pleached trees: for above-fence privacy
  • Pergolas with climbers: for overhead privacy
  • Screens: timber or metal screens

See our privacy design guide.

5. Fire Pit or Heater

A fire pit or outdoor heater extends the entertaining season into the cooler months:

  • Fire pit: wood-burning, 100 to 500 pounds
  • Fire table: gas, 500 to 2,000 pounds
  • Patio heater: freestanding, 100 to 500 pounds
  • Infrared heater: wall-mounted, 200 to 800 pounds

How to Design an Entertaining Garden

  1. Plan the zones: decide where the dining, lounging, and cooking zones will be
  2. Choose the patio size: allow at least 4 by 4 metres for a dining area
  3. Install power points: for lighting, heaters, and speakers
  4. Install lighting: plan the lighting scheme before laying the patio
  5. Provide privacy: plant hedges or install screens
  6. Choose comfortable furniture: that fits the space
  7. Add a fire feature: for cool evenings
  8. Install outdoor speakers: for music

Common Mistakes

  • Not planning the zones. A patio with everything crammed together does not work for entertaining.
  • Not installing power. You need power for lighting, heaters, and speakers. Plan it before laying the patio.
  • Not providing privacy. Entertaining is not enjoyable if neighbours can see in.
  • Not installing lighting. Evening entertaining needs good lighting.
  • Buying cheap furniture. Cheap outdoor furniture does not last. Invest in quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

How big should an entertaining patio be?

At least 4 by 4 metres for a dining area, larger if you want separate zones for dining and lounging.

Should I have an outdoor kitchen?

If you entertain a lot, yes. A basic outdoor kitchen (built-in barbecue and worktop) costs 1,000 to 3,000 pounds.

What is the best lighting for an entertaining garden?

A combination: string lights for ambience, path lights for safety, uplighting on trees for drama, and candles on tables.
