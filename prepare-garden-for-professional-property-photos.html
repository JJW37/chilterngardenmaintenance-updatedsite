The short answer

Quick Answer

To prepare a garden for professional property photos: mow and edge the lawn 1 to 2 days before, tidy all visible areas, clean the patio, plant a few pots for colour, open parasols and garden furniture, and choose a sunny day for the photos. Cost: 100 to 400 pounds. The photos sell the property, so the garden needs to look its best.

Why Garden Photos Matter

Professional property photos are the first thing buyers see on Rightmove and Zoopla. They determine whether a buyer clicks through to view the property. The garden photos are particularly important: they show the size, the usability, and the condition of the garden. A well-prepared garden photographs well and attracts buyers. A neglected garden photographs badly and puts buyers off.

In the Chilterns, where gardens are a major selling point, the photos are critical. A good garden photo can add thousands to the sale price. A bad garden photo can lose viewings. This guide explains how to prepare a garden for professional property photos.

The 8-Step Photo Preparation

1. Mow and Edge the Lawn (1-2 days before)

Mow the lawn 1 to 2 days before the photos, not the day of. This gives the grass time to settle and the clippings time to disperse. Mow at 30 to 40mm. Edge along paths and borders with a half-moon edger.

2. Tidy All Visible Areas

Weed the borders, deadhead flowering plants, sweep paths and patios. Remove all rubbish and clutter. The visible areas (patio, lawn, borders near the house) should look immaculate.

3. Clean the Patio

Sweep the patio, then pressure wash if needed. A clean patio looks bigger and more inviting in photos. See our pressure washing service.

4. Plant Pots for Colour

Place a few pots of seasonal colour by the doors and on the patio. This adds life and colour to the photos. Avoid too many pots, which look cluttered.

5. Set Up Garden Furniture

Set up garden furniture: table and chairs on the patio, parasol up if sunny. This helps buyers imagine using the garden. Arrange the furniture as if for a meal.

6. Hide Wheelie Bins and Clutter

Move wheelie bins, children's toys, garden tools, and empty pots out of sight. A clutter-free garden photographs better.

7. Choose the Right Day

Choose a sunny day for the photos. Sun makes the garden look bright and inviting. Avoid overcast days if possible. If the photos must be taken on an overcast day, ask the photographer to use editing to brighten the images.

8. Time of Day

The best time for garden photos is mid-morning (10 to 11 am) or mid-afternoon (3 to 4 pm), when the sun is at a good angle. Avoid midday, when the sun is overhead and creates harsh shadows.

What Photographers Look For

  • Clean, tidy garden: the garden should look cared for
  • Defined zones: patio, lawn, borders
  • Garden furniture: helps buyers imagine using the garden
  • Colour: pots of flowers, flowering shrubs
  • Light: sun in the garden, especially on the patio
  • Space: the garden should look bigger than it is

Common Mistakes

  • Mowing on the day of the photos. Clippings get everywhere. Mow 1 to 2 days before.
  • Not cleaning the patio. A dirty patio looks bad in photos. Sweep and pressure wash.
  • Not setting up furniture. Empty patios look uninviting. Set up table and chairs.
  • Leaving clutter in view. Wheelie bins, toys, and tools ruin the photos. Hide them.
  • Taking photos on an overcast day. Sun makes the garden look better. Wait for a sunny day if possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time for garden photos?

Mid-morning (10 to 11 am) or mid-afternoon (3 to 4 pm) on a sunny day. Avoid midday (harsh shadows) and overcast days (flat light).

Should I mow on the day of the photos?

No. Mow 1 to 2 days before. This gives the grass time to settle and the clippings time to disperse.

What should I do if the photos are on an overcast day?

Make sure the garden is extra tidy, as the light will be flat. Ask the photographer to use editing to brighten the images. If possible, reschedule for a sunny day.
