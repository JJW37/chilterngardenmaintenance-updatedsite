Why Storm Damage Checklists Matter

Storms can cause significant damage to gardens. Fallen trees, broken fences, damaged patios, and split plants are all common after a storm. The key is to assess the damage quickly, prioritise the repairs, and document everything for insurance. This guide gives a storm damage garden checklist, so you know what to check and in what order.

In the Chilterns, where winter storms (November to February) are common and many gardens have mature trees, storm damage is a regular occurrence. This checklist is based on what we see after storms across Oxfordshire, Buckinghamshire, Berkshire and Bedfordshire.

The 8-Point Storm Damage Checklist

1. Check for Fallen Trees and Branches

Walk the garden and check for:

  • Fallen trees
  • Broken branches (hanging or on the ground)
  • Leaning trees
  • Split trunks

If a tree has fallen on a path, building, or power line, call a tree surgeon immediately. Do not try to remove it yourself.

2. Check Fences and Structures

Check for:

  • Fallen fence panels
  • Broken fence posts
  • Leaning gates
  • Damaged sheds and outbuildings
  • Broken trellis and arches

See our fencing service for repairs.

3. Check the Roof and Gutters

Check for:

  • Missing or loose tiles
  • Blocked gutters (leaves and debris)
  • Loose aerials and chimneys
  • Damage to fascias and soffits

4. Check Paths and Patios

Check for:

  • Loose paving slabs
  • Cracked patios
  • Sunken areas
  • Blocked drains

5. Check Plants for Damage

Check for:

  • Broken stems on shrubs and perennials
  • Split shrubs
  • Leaning plants
  • Wind-rocked plants (lifted roots)
  • Frost damage (blackened leaves)

Prune damaged growth to prevent disease.

6. Check Ponds and Water Features

Check for:

  • Debris in the pond
  • Blocked pumps
  • Damaged liners
  • Flooded ponds

7. Check Garden Furniture

Check for:

  • Blown-over furniture
  • Broken tables and chairs
  • Damaged parasols
  • Displaced pots and containers

8. Document Everything for Insurance

If you plan to claim on insurance:

  • Take photos of all damage
  • Note the date and time of the storm
  • Keep receipts for emergency repairs
  • Contact your insurer before starting major repairs

The Priority Order for Repairs

  1. Safety: fallen trees on paths, buildings, or power lines
  2. Weatherproofing: damaged roofs, gutters, and windows
  3. Security: broken fences and gates
  4. Plant care: prune damaged plants, stake leaning plants
  5. Structural: damaged paths, patios, and outbuildings
  6. Cosmetic: minor damage that does not affect safety or function

Common Mistakes

  • Not checking for hanging branches. Hanging branches can fall and injure people. Remove them immediately.
  • Trying to remove large trees yourself. Large tree removal is dangerous. Hire a qualified tree surgeon.
  • Not documenting damage for insurance. Take photos before starting repairs.
  • Not pruning damaged plants. Damaged plants are vulnerable to disease. Prune them promptly.
  • Not checking ponds and water features. Debris in ponds can kill fish and wildlife. Clean them out.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will my insurance cover storm damage to the garden?

Usually yes, but check your policy. Most policies cover fences, sheds, and outbuildings, but may not cover plants.

Should I try to remove a fallen tree myself?

No, if it is large or has fallen on a building, path, or power line. Hire a qualified tree surgeon. Small trees (under 5cm trunk diameter) can be removed yourself.

How do I prevent storm damage in the future?

Prune trees regularly, replace loose fence posts, secure garden furniture, and check gutters before winter.
